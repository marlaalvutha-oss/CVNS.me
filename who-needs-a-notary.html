Who Needs A Notary?

Anyone signing a legal or financial document, such as a will, deed, or power of attorney, needs a notary to verify their identity and witness the signature. This includes individuals in a wide range of situations, from buying a home to establishing an estate plan, as well as businesses that need to notarize contracts, loan agreements, and other important paperwork.

For Individuals

  • Estate Planning: Wills, living wills, and powers of attorney
  • Real Estate: Property deeds, mortgage documents, and leasing agreements
  • Financial Matters: Loan documents and insurance claims
  • Legal and Personal: Affidavits, sworn statements, and name change documents
  • Travel: Travel consent forms for minors
  • Vehicle Transfers: Title and bill of sale forms for cars, motorcycles, or boats
  • Immigration: Certain forms, such as affidavits or sworn statements, for applications

For Businesses

  • Financial Services: Banks, insurance agencies, and mortgage companies often require notarization for loan documents and claims
  • Real Estate: Real estate agencies need notarization for deeds, permits, and leasing agreements
  • Automotive: Car dealerships need it for title transfers and purchase agreements
  • Contracts: Any business, regardless of size, may need a notary for partnership agreements, loan contracts, and other business contracts
  • Leasing: Equipment leasing companies use notarization for their contracts
  • Other Industries: Construction companies, schools, and corporations of all types

Virginia Notary Requirements & Limitations

  • A notary is a public official appointed by the Governor. Even though a notary may hold that position to serve his or her employer, no employer or supervisor can impose requirements pertaining to a notary that are contrary to Virginia's laws and regulations pertaining to notaries.
  • Valid photo identification is required for all notarizations.
  • A Virginia notary cannot certify birth certificates, marriage certificates, or death certificates (copies or originals).
  • A Virginia notary is not authorized to perform marriages or any act that constitutes the practice of law.
  • A Virginia notary may notarize a document to be filed and used outside of Virginia if it is signed and notarized in Virginia.
